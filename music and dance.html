Dances & Music

Mokhibo (Dance)

A traditional women’s dance performed kneeling, with rhythmic shoulder movements and clapping to create harmony and energy.

Mohobelo (Dance)

A men’s dance characterized by stomping and synchronized movements, performed during celebrations and communal events.

Likhomo (Dance)

A celebratory dance often performed at weddings, involving call-and-response songs and lively steps.

Setolotolo (Instrument)

A traditional mouth harp made from metal, producing twangy sounds used for self-expression and storytelling.

Thomo (Instrument)

A women’s stringed instrument made from a gourd and horsehair strings, often played during domestic gatherings.

Lesiba (Instrument)

A wind instrument played by herders, producing flute-like sounds that mimic nature and communicate across distances.

Mokhibo (Dance)

A women’s dance performed kneeling, with rhythmic shoulder movements, clapping, and chants.

Mohobelo (Dance)

A men’s stomping dance with synchronized steps, often performed at initiation ceremonies.

Mokorotlo (Dance)

A celebratory war dance accompanied by ululations and stamping, symbolizing bravery.

Likhomo (Dance)

A joyful dance performed during weddings, with call-and-response singing and lively movements.

Sehlotšo (Dance)

A harvest celebration dance involving playful mimicry and fast-paced steps.

Letšoao (Dance)

A coming-of-age dance used to mark initiation, featuring rhythmic chants and gestures.

Thotha (Dance)

A traditional dance where men jump high to showcase agility and strength.

Khau (Dance)

A victory dance symbolizing honor and achievement, often seen after communal competitions.

Lefela (Dance)

A circle dance where participants move in unity, reinforcing social bonds.

Lehopo (Dance)

A ritual dance performed during spiritual gatherings to invoke ancestral blessings.

Setolotolo (Instrument)

A mouth harp producing twangy sounds, often used for personal expression and storytelling.

Lesiba (Instrument)

A wind instrument played by herders, imitating bird calls and natural sounds.

Thomo (Instrument)

A stringed gourd instrument played by women, associated with domestic gatherings.

Moropa (Drum)

Traditional drums used to mark rhythm and summon communities for ceremonies.

Phala (Horn)

An animal horn blown to announce events, signal danger, or start dances.

Khudu (Rattle)

A percussion instrument made from seeds or shells, shaken during dances for rhythm.

Lengope (Whistle)

A whistle used in dances to coordinate performers and signal transitions.
